I’m a third-year Computer Engineering student at the University of Michigan. Whether I’m exploring new ideas on my own or helping others understand something alongside me, I see learning as one of the most valuable opportunities we have. It’s what drives progress, creativity, and us as people. The more I learn, the more I realize how vast and fascinating the world of knowledge truly is. I’m grateful for the opportunities I’ve had to develop new skills, apply what I’ve learned, and continue growing through each challenge. My goal is to stay on that path for the long term: always curious, always building, and always learning.

I’m currently pursuing my Bachelor’s degree in Computer Engineering, with an expected graduation date of December 2027, and I plan to continue at the University of Michigan to earn an ECE Master’s degree, expected in December 2028. I’m excited to keep building on my academic foundation while deepening my technical expertise through advanced coursework, research, and hands-on design experience.

My interests center around mixed-signal and digital hardware design, with a focus on VLSI, RTL design, processor architecture, physical design, and verification. I’ve been fortunate to contribute to several large-scale projects that have strengthened both my technical skills and appreciation for end-to-end hardware development. I’m always optimistic about new opportunities to learn, grow, and collaborate, so please feel free to connect with me!
